RAAD Research Project

The Healthy Opportunities Pilot (HOP) program, started by the North Carolina Department of Health and Human Services is an initiative to test and evaluate the impact of offering targeted, evidence-based, non-medical interventions focused on housing, food, transportation, interpersonal safety, and toxic stress for high-needs Medicaid enrollees. Me and my colleagues, Gracen Alleman and Neylan Visnius, worked with Bounty & Soul, a local non-profit, to evaluate which specific areas within 3 counties in Western North Carolina (Buncombe, McDowell, and Henderson) may be more vunerable in terms of health and socioeconomic factors where they may be eligible to recieve fresh produce provided by Bounty & Soul.
